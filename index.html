About

Dr Brian Devlin is an Adjunct Professor at Charles Darwin University, known for his work in bilingual education, applied linguistics, and Indigenous language policy. He is also a published poet with deep connections to the Northern Territory and to the Gumatj clan.

  • Languages: English, Tok Pisin, Gumatj, Chinese, Russian
  • Education:
    • EdD, Columbia University
    • MEd, Columbia University
    • Advanced Course in Linguistics, Summer Institute of Linguistics
    • DipEd, Canberra College of Advanced Education
    • BA (Hons), University of Melbourne
  • Clan Adoption: Gumatj clan, 1979

From "Under the Night Sky"

Chagall – Brian Devlin

Chagall

Take Vitebsk.
Lovers toddle into flowers.
One very nude lady drifts apprehensively above field and church,
Recalling the cunning buoyancy of that fabled cow.
And Christ flies down from the cross with gaudy wings
To drink with friends on festival days.
Carlton loft
1960s

From "No Cold and Paltry Contest"

Due anime di farfalla – Brian Devlin

Due anime di farfalla

We met in innocent trust,
in truth and understanding,
before ever there was a kiss,
just like butterfly souls,
but did we do that as eggs,
in that initial stage
when butterflies begin their lives?
Surely not.
Were we at that time
like larvae growing rapidly,
happily munching on leaves
and other plant matter
for what we thought would be the rest of our lives?
Surely not that either.
Were we then
young pupae or chrysalides
on their third stage of transformation
when we first met?
If so, we must have been
wrapped in veils of silk,
and was our entering into
a trembling, new awareness
the same state that others well understand
and conventionally describe,
or was it our very own epiphany.
for which there are no words?
We did emerge later,
fully formed with wings,
but when was that?
Our sophisticated pinhead brains
and optic lobes
guide us as we detect colours,
patterns and movement,
but they somehow failed to register
our glide into becoming,
our breathtaking leap into transformative being,
not to mention
these final moments
of our unbecoming,
which are now upon us.
— Brian Devlin
Darwin, May 4th, 2024
